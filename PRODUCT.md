# Pelagos Subsea

## Register

Brand

## Product Purpose

Pelagos Subsea is a fictional marine robotics company that builds quiet, modular autonomous vehicles for reef mapping and coastal science. The website gives research teams and conservation partners an immediate sense of the vehicle in its environment, explains its practical capabilities, and invites expedition planning conversations.

The experience should demonstrate technical confidence without resembling a generic software landing page. The submersible is the central object and the ocean is the interface.

## Primary Users

- Marine scientists planning reef or seagrass surveys.
- Conservation organizations documenting habitat change.
- Coastal authorities and engineering teams needing repeatable underwater data.
- Research vessel operators evaluating compact autonomous platforms.

## User Goals

1. Understand what the Asteria vehicle does and where it can operate.
2. Explore its form and sensor payload in an interactive scene.
3. Review range, depth, mapping, and environmental-impact details.
4. Start a scoped expedition conversation.

## Brand Identity

Pelagos is observant, buoyant, and field-hardened. It values useful measurements, repairable equipment, quiet propulsion, and responsible access to fragile habitats.

Voice words: submersed, precise, alive.

## Offering

The fictional Asteria autonomous vehicle is a compact electric platform built for shallow and mesophotic work. It supports photogrammetry, multibeam sonar, water-quality sampling, and repeatable transects down to 300 meters. Its modular shell and low-noise thrusters reduce operational footprint around wildlife.

## Content Principles

- Speak in direct field language, not speculative tech hype.
- Connect every capability to a research outcome.
- Make sustainability concrete through quiet operation, repairability, and repeatable monitoring.
- Never use fake testimonials, fabricated partner logos, or invented scientific results.
- Never use em dashes.

## Core Content

- Hero: see what the reef is becoming.
- Interactive Asteria vehicle and reef scene.
- Capabilities: map, sample, return.
- Mission profile: 300 m depth, 18 h endurance, 0.4 m navigation repeatability.
- Approach: low acoustic signature and modular field repair.
- Expedition CTA.

## Functional Requirements

- One-page responsive site.
- Immersive WebGL or high-quality pseudo-3D ocean scene.
- Pointer-steerable or orbiting submersible with clearly optional interaction.
- Accessible semantic DOM overlay containing all essential content.
- Reduced-motion and WebGL-failure fallbacks.
- Clear expedition inquiry CTA.
- Keyboard-visible focus states and sufficient contrast.

## Success Criteria

- The interactive vehicle feels integrated with the page, not embedded as a demo panel.
- Visitors can state the depth, endurance, and primary mission use after scanning.
- Technical content stays readable over the scene on desktop and mobile.
- Animation remains smooth and does not trigger React re-renders per frame.

## Anti-References

- Neon cyan software dashboards and generic space-tech landing pages.
- Floating glass cards over a dark blue gradient.
- Stock photos used as scientific proof.
- Feature-card grids with repeated icon-heading-copy patterns.
- Invented impact statistics or partner endorsements.
